Sacred Areas in Sinai

Discover the holy sites and spiritual locations where the Holy Family passed through during their journey to Egypt

Mount Sinai

The sacred mountain where Moses received the Ten Commandments. This holy site has been a place of pilgrimage for centuries and holds deep significance in the Holy Family's journey through the Sinai Peninsula. The mountain's spiritual atmosphere and breathtaking views continue to inspire visitors from around the world.

Explore

Saint Catherine's Monastery

One of the oldest continuously operating Christian monasteries in the world, built at the foot of Mount Sinai. This UNESCO World Heritage site houses an incredible collection of ancient manuscripts and icons, preserving the Christian heritage that connects to the Holy Family's time in Egypt.

Explore

Wadi Feiran

Known as the "Pearl of Sinai," this oasis valley is believed to be one of the routes taken by the Holy Family. The area features palm groves, ancient ruins, and a serene atmosphere that provides insight into the landscape the Holy Family would have encountered during their journey.

Explore

Raithu

An ancient Christian settlement in southern Sinai that served as an important stop for pilgrims and travelers. This historic site represents the early Christian presence in the region and connects to the broader story of Christianity's spread throughout Egypt during the time of the Holy Family.
